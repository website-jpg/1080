1080's Effect On Water

1080 is used in bushy, hard-to-reach areas to make pest-control more efficient. These areas often include bodies of water, which raises the question of whether these lakes are now poisoned, and pose a health risk to aquatic life, animals who may drink the these bodies of water, and potentially our residential water source, from the natural movement of water from one body to another, to where our drinking water is sourced from. The Department of Food and Agriculture Australia published a report looking into addressing common concerns about 1080.

This source can be trusted as a valid source, as it is published by the Australian Government which is a reputable source, and presents only factual information, which therefore means there is no bias. It states: ‘1080 does not attain harmful levels and/or persist in waterways, even when quite high natural concentrations of fluoroacetate are present in the surrounding environment.’

This provided me with information that explains how 1080 persists in water. 1080 doesn’t persist in water, meaning it breaks down into harmless products in the water. New Zealand Ecology backs this information up with additional information on how 1080 manifests in water. ‘1080 derived from baits will also be dispersed by water since it is highly water soluble and mobile.’ - New Zealand Ecology. The same source, New Zealand Ecology, presents more information regarding the topic, ‘Unlike wellknown environmental EDCs like DDT or dioxins, 1080 and fluorocitrate are simple molecules, which are water soluble and do not persist for long in the environment.’ This source provides trustworthy information, as all the reports provided by this New Zealand organisation are written by qualified scientists and are peer-reviewed.
